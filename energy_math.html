The potential energy of the system is PE = −GMmr and the kinetic energy is KE = 12 mv2. The total energy is

E = 12 mv02 − GMmR0 = −GMmR

where R is the maximum height and R0 is the start radius (e.g. surface of planet). This allows for the velocity to be solve as a function of PE.

v = ±( 2m ( E − PE ))12

Solving this for t using v = drdt and using the positive of the ± (and rearranging for the purposes of sign) gives

t = ∫R0R drv = ( m−2E )12 ∫R0R dr ( PEE − 1 )12 = ( m−2E )12 ∫R0r dr ( Rr − 1 )12

where the last terms uses PEE= Rr. This integrates to the lovely expression:

t = ( m−2E )12 ( −rRr − 1 − Rtanh−1 ( Rr − 1 ) |R0R )

giving the final time of rise to height R to be

tR = ( m−2E )12 ( R0RR0 − 1 + Rtanh−1 ( RR0 − 1 ) )

The equation, however, does not invert analytically.
